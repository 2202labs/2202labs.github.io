Problem

Manual and disconnected handling creates avoidable effort across the full process.

Timesheet handling often becomes harder than it needs to be when submission, review, approval, and reporting are spread across manual steps or disconnected tools. In practice, this creates repetitive effort, inconsistent handling, avoidable follow-up, and limited visibility for both end users and reviewers.

Context

The goal is to redesign the workflow, not just swap out the current tooling.

This work is based on a real operational pain point experienced from the end-user side of an internal timesheet process that currently depends heavily on Excel, VBA, and manual handling. The goal is not just to replace one tool with another, but to redesign the workflow so that submission, approval, and downstream reporting become more structured, traceable, and easier to operate.

What I Built

A Python-based workflow tool focused on how timesheet data moves through the process.

I worked on building a Python-based workflow tool that focuses on how timesheet data moves through the full process: user submission, reviewer validation, approval handling, status visibility, and reporting preparation. The emphasis is on improving workflow structure and reducing manual friction rather than building a feature-heavy application.

Workflow Focus

The work is centered on a few practical operational questions.

  • How can submission be made simpler and less error-prone for end users?
  • How can approval handling become more structured and visible?
  • How can downstream reporting become easier to prepare and review?
  • How can the workflow reduce repetitive manual coordination without becoming harder to maintain?

Design Direction

The implementation treats the problem as workflow and state management.

The implementation treats the problem primarily as a workflow and state-management challenge rather than only a form-entry problem. That means focusing on cleaner handoffs, clearer status visibility, validation at the right stages, and automation where it improves reliability and reduces manual effort.

Why It Matters

A better timesheet process improves accuracy, tracking, and day-to-day usability.

A better timesheet process is not just about convenience. It improves accuracy, reduces avoidable administrative effort, makes review and approval easier to track, and creates a more usable operational flow for the people involved in the process day to day.

Current Status

The implementation direction is established and still actively being refined.

This is an active portfolio project with a working implementation direction already established. The current value lies in the workflow structure, operating flow, and automation thinking behind the tool, with room to continue refining validation, reporting, and user experience.

Next Improvements

Further work is focused on clearer stages, validation, visibility, and maintainability.

  • Refine the end-to-end workflow stages more explicitly
  • Define the validation and exception-handling paths more clearly
  • Improve status tracking for submitters and approvers
  • Clarify how reporting outputs should be generated from approved records
  • Keep the implementation simple enough to remain maintainable over time
