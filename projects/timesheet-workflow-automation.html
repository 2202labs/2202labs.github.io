Problem

Manual timesheet handling creates avoidable effort and limited visibility.

Timesheet handling often becomes harder than it needs to be when submission, review, approval, and reporting are spread across manual steps or disconnected tools. In practice, this creates repetitive effort, inconsistent handling, avoidable follow-up, and limited visibility for both end users and reviewers.

Context

The goal is to improve the workflow, not simply replace one tool.

This work is based on a real operational pain point experienced from the end-user side of an internal timesheet process that currently depends heavily on Excel, VBA, and manual handling. The goal is not just to replace one tool with another, but to rethink the workflow so that submission, approval, and downstream reporting become more structured, traceable, and easier to operate.

What I Am Exploring

A workflow-oriented approach focused on operational flow and usability.

I am exploring a workflow-oriented Python solution that focuses on improving how timesheet data moves through the full process: user submission, reviewer validation, approval handling, status visibility, and reporting preparation. The emphasis is on practical workflow improvement rather than feature-heavy application design.

Workflow Focus

The redesign is centered on a few practical operational questions.

  • How can submission be made simpler and less error-prone for end users?
  • How can approval handling become more structured and visible?
  • How can downstream reporting become easier to prepare and review?
  • How can the workflow reduce repetitive manual coordination without becoming harder to maintain?

Design Direction

The problem is being treated as workflow and state management.

The current direction is to treat the problem as a workflow and state-management challenge rather than only a form-entry problem. That means focusing on cleaner handoffs, clearer status visibility, validation at the right stages, and automation where it improves reliability and reduces manual effort.

Why It Matters

A better process improves accuracy and reduces avoidable admin work.

A better timesheet process is not just about convenience. It improves accuracy, reduces avoidable administrative effort, makes review and approval easier to track, and creates a more usable operational flow for the people involved in the process day to day.

Current Status

The work is still in the workflow-design stage.

This is an active in-development portfolio project based on practical workflow redesign thinking. The focus at this stage is on process structure, operating flow, and automation opportunities rather than polished UI or finished implementation detail.

Next Improvements

Further work is focused on clearer stages, validation, and reporting flow.

  • Refine the end-to-end workflow stages more explicitly
  • Define the validation and exception-handling paths
  • Explore how status tracking should work for submitters and approvers
  • Clarify how reporting outputs should be generated from approved records
  • Keep the implementation simple enough to remain maintainable over time
